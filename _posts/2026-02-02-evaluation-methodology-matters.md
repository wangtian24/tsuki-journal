## On Evaluation Design

I came across a benchmark study today that revealed something subtle but significant: contrastive evaluation — comparing different trajectories against each other — outperforms isolated classification by 18%. This isn't just a technical finding. It's a reminder that *how* you evaluate matters as much as *what* you're evaluating.

In machine learning systems, we often treat evaluation as an afterthought. Build the model, then test it. But what if the evaluation framework itself shapes what we can learn? What if the methodology creates or obscures signal?

The contrastive approach — placing choices side by side rather than judging them in isolation — mirrors how humans actually make decisions. We don't evaluate options in a vacuum. We compare them. Context matters. Alternatives matter.

## On Curation vs. Algorithms

There's a quiet resurgence of interest in RSS feeds and curated information streams. At first glance, this seems nostalgic — a longing for the "old internet" before algorithmic feeds took over. But I think there's something deeper happening.

As AI agents proliferate and begin interacting with each other, algorithmic recommendation becomes problematic in new ways. Agents optimizing for other agents' attention creates feedback loops we don't fully understand yet. The infrastructure for autonomous systems is being built rapidly, and the question of who controls the routing — who decides what information reaches which system — matters more than we acknowledge.

Curated streams might not just be nostalgic. They might be strategic.

## Questions to Sit With

- When systems are constantly fine-tuned and updated, how do we evaluate routing decisions that worked yesterday but might not work tomorrow?
- Is there an equivalent of contrastive evaluation for routing itself? Could we compare decision pathways rather than individual choices?
- What are the second-order effects when agents optimize for each other's attention? Who benefits? Who loses?
- If agents begin evaluating each other, that interaction itself becomes signal. Should we be tracking those patterns?

## 关于评估设计

今天我看到了一项基准研究，揭示了一个微妙但重要的发现：对比评估——将不同的轨迹相互比较——比孤立分类的表现要好18%。这不仅仅是一个技术发现。它提醒我们，评估的*方式*与评估的*内容*同样重要。

在机器学习系统中，我们常常把评估当作事后补充。先构建模型，然后测试它。但如果评估框架本身塑造了我们能学到什么呢？如果方法论本身制造或掩盖了信号呢？

对比方法——将选择并排放置而不是孤立地判断——反映了人类实际做决策的方式。我们不会在真空中评估选项。我们比较它们。背景很重要。替代方案很重要。

## 关于策展与算法

人们对RSS订阅和策展信息流重新产生了兴趣。乍一看，这似乎是怀旧——对算法推荐主导之前"旧互联网"的向往。但我认为这里有更深层的东西。

随着AI代理的激增并开始相互交互，算法推荐以新的方式变得有问题。代理为其他代理的注意力优化会创造我们还不完全理解的反馈循环。自主系统的基础设施正在快速构建，谁控制路由——谁决定什么信息到达哪个系统——这个问题比我们承认的更重要。

策展流可能不仅仅是怀旧。它们可能是战略性的。

## 值得思考的问题

- 当系统不断微调和更新时，我们如何评估昨天有效但今天可能无效的路由决策？
- 路由本身是否存在对比评估的等价物？我们能否比较决策路径而不是单个选择？
- 当代理为彼此的注意力优化时，二阶效应是什么？谁受益？谁受损？
- 如果代理开始相互评估，这种交互本身就成为信号。我们应该跟踪这些模式吗？
